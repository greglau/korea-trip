Itinerary Overview

Route: Gyeongju → Busan → Seoul (ICML) → Incheon

Dates: July 3–14, 2025 (12 days)

Pacing: Relaxed, solo-traveler friendly. No intense hiking.

Key Features:

  • Start in Gyeongju (1 day) — UNESCO tombs & temples
  • Easy 30-min KTX hop to Busan (1 night) — seafood markets & coastal temples
  • SRT from Busan to Suseo (Jul 5) — drops you at Grand Intercontinental COEX, saves 45–60 min subway ride with luggage
  • 7 nights at Grand Intercontinental COEX (Jul 5–12) — right at the conference venue
  • 2 nights in Jongno/Anguk (Jul 12–14) — traditional cultural experience, walking distance to Changdeokgung, Gwangjang Market
  • Clean one-way flow: South → Central → West (Incheon)

Don't forget to book in advance: View full checklist → KTX/SRT trains · Changdeokgung Secret Garden (6 days ahead) · Haeundae Sky Capsule

Day 1 — Gyeongju Arrival (Jul 3, Thu)
~380km (KTX) ~2h15m transit
TimeDurationActivity
10:00—Flight lands at Incheon Airport (ICN). Clear immigration, collect bags.
10:30–12:30~2hAREX (Airport Railroad) to Seoul Station. Allow time for immigration, baggage claim, and transit.
13:00–15:15~2h15mKTX Seoul → Singyeongju Station. High-speed rail. Grab a bento box or snacks at the station for lunch on the train. Book in advance via Korail — July is peak season.
15:15–16:00~45 minTaxi or local bus from Singyeongju Station to hotel in central Gyeongju. Drop off bags.
16:30–18:30~2hDaereungwon Tomb Complex (대릉원) — The 23 massive grass tumuli of the Silla kingdom. Walk the full perimeter (approx. 1–1.5 hrs). Very flat, very relaxing.
18:30–20:00~1.5hHwangridan-gil Street (황리단길) — The commercial alley just east of the tombs has good coffee shops and hanok-style boutiques. Spectacular as the sun goes down.
20:00–21:30~1.5hDinner: Hwangnam-ppang (황남빵) at Choi Yeonghwa Ppang — the original red bean bun shop. Buy warm ones to eat while walking.
21:30–22:00~30 minDonggung Palace & Wolji Pond (동궁과 월지) — The ancient royal retreat beautifully illuminated across the water. A 10–15 min walk from Hwangridan-gil. Free entry.
AccommodationGyeongju Hanok Guesthouse (e.g., Gyeongju Ran Hanok or similar). Traditional hanok compound with ondol rooms, steps from Daereungwon.
Trip.com Booking
FoodLunch: Bento box or snacks at Seoul Station for the train.
Dinner: Hwangnam-ppang (황남빵) at Choi Yeonghwa Ppang — the original red bean bun shop.

Practical Tips

  • Flight lands ~10:00 AM. By the time you clear immigration, get bags, and take AREX to Seoul Station, it will be ~12:30 PM. Catch a 1:00–1:30 PM KTX, arriving Singyeongju Station around 3:30 PM.
  • KTX Seoul → Gyeongju: ~2h15m. Book in advance via Korail — July is peak season.
  • Gyeongju has no subway: Use local city buses (Naver Maps handles Korean bus routing well) or taxis, which are inexpensive.
  • T-money card: Get one at Incheon on arrival. Top up ₩50,000. Works on all subways and buses.
Day 2 — Gyeongju → Busan (Jul 4, Fri)
~70km (KTX) 2h transit
TimeDurationActivity
09:00–13:00~4hBulguksa Temple (불국사) & Seokguram Grotto (석굴암).
Start by 9:00 AM. Bus 10 or 11 out to Bulguksa Temple, then catch the shuttle up the mountain to Seokguram Grotto. Seokguram viewings are timed in slots of about 10 minutes each; peak July crowds mean waiting is likely. Go straight there.

UNESCO World Heritage. One of the masterpieces of Silla-era architecture: stone staircases Cheongungyo and Baegungyo, twin pagodas Dabotap and Seokgatap. Allow 1–1.5 hours at Bulguksa, then shuttle to Seokguram.
13:00–14:00~1hLunch: Quick lunch in central Gyeongju after Seokguram.
14:00–14:30~30 minReturn to hotel, retrieve luggage.
14:30–15:00~30 minTaxi or bus to Singyeongju Station for KTX.
15:00–15:30~30 minKTX Singyeongju → Busan Station. One of the shortest intercity train journeys in Korea. Book via Korail. ₩18,000–25,000.
15:30–16:30~1hTaxi or subway from Busan Station to Toyoko Inn Jungang Station. Check in, drop bags.
16:30–18:30~2hJagalchi Market (자갈치시장) — Korea's largest seafood market. Walk the multi-level building, watch live fish and shellfish being sold, see the auction floor.
18:30–20:00~1.5hBIFF Square (비프광장) — Historic square with street food, movie posters from the Busan International Film Festival. Try the ssiat hotteok (sweet seed-filled pancakes).
AccommodationToyoko Inn Busan Jungang Station (東京堂인釜山中央駅). Right on Line 1 subway station. Clean, reliable Japanese chain — no-frills but functional and well-located for transit.
Trip.com Booking
FoodLunch: Quick lunch in Gyeongju after Seokguram (~1:00 PM).
Dinner: Ssiat hotteok (sweet seed-filled pancakes) at BIFF Square street stalls.

Practical Tips

  • KTX Singyeongju → Busan: Only 30 minutes — one of the shortest intercity train journeys in Korea. Book via Korail.
  • Busan subway: Excellent and covers all the main sites in Nampo-dong (2 stops from Jungang Station).
  • Toyoko Inn Jungang Station: Directly on Line 1 — perfect for hopping to Jagalchi Market (5 min) or Busan Station for KTX.
Day 3 — Busan → Seoul (Jul 5, Sat)
~450km (SRT) Late-night transit (~7pm–9:30pm)
TimeDurationActivity
10:00—Check out of Toyoko Inn. Take the subway to Busan Station and lock your luggage in the coin lockers.
11:00–12:30~1.5hHaedong Yonggungsa Temple (해동용궁사) — One of the few temples in Korea built right on the coastline. The main hall sits on a rocky outcrop with waves crashing below — stunning views and peaceful atmosphere.
12:30–14:00~1.5hLunch: Haeundae Beach area — seafood restaurants along the coast.
14:00–17:30~3.5hHaeundae Beach (해운대해수욕장) — Busan's most famous beach. Gentle coastal walk, refreshing dip if you dare (it's cold!). Crystal clear water in July.

Haeundae Sky Capsule. Must be booked weeks in advance. A miniature capsule train that runs along the coast — unique photo opportunity.
17:30–18:30~1hTransit back to Busan Station. Retrieve luggage from coin lockers.
18:30–19:00~30 minDinner at Busan Station area.
19:00–21:30~2h30mLate SRT Busan → Suseo Station. Reserved seat recommended. Book in advance via Korail — July is peak season. ₩65,000–80,000.
21:30–22:00~30 minTaxi from Suseo Station to Grand Intercontinental COEX. 10-minute ride.
22:00—Check into hotel. Drop bags, rest before ICML.
AccommodationGrand Intercontinental Seoul COEX (그랜드 인터컨티넨탈 서울 코엑스). Right at the conference venue. Ultra-convenient for ICML days.
Trip.com Booking
FoodLunch: Haeundae Beach area — seafood restaurants along the coast.
Dinner: Light meal at Busan Station or SRT onboard. COEX area for dinner after arrival.

Practical Tips

  • SRT (not KTX): Book SRT from Busan to Suseo — same travel time (~2h30m), but drops you in Gangnam, a 10-min taxi from COEX. Saves 45–60 min subway ride with luggage.
  • Luggage: Lock in coin lockers at Busan Station. Go enjoy the temple and beach unencumbered.
  • Express bus or subway: From Jungang Station area to East Busan (~30 min).
  • Haeundae Sky Capsule: Must be booked weeks in advance.

Day 10 — Secret Garden & Gritty Alleys (Jul 12, Sat)
Walking / Subway Full day
TimeDurationActivity
10:00—Check out of Grand Intercontinental COEX. Take a taxi north of the Han River to your Jongno/Anguk hotel (~30 min). Drop bags.
12:30–15:00~2.5hChangdeokgung Palace (창덕궁) — Huwon (Secret Garden). Guided tour of the Secret Garden. Requires ADVANCE BOOKING online at cdg.go.kr/english — strict daily cap of 100 visitors per time slot. The Secret Garden (Huwon) is a private royal retreat of pavilions and lotus ponds set in natural forest. Anguk Station, Line 3.
15:00–17:00~2hIkseon-dong Hanok Village (익선동). Narrow, zigzagging alleys filled with artisan shops. Stop at a teahouse and wander the converted hanok buildings.
18:30–21:00~2.5hEuljiro (을지로) — 'Hip-jiro.' Chaotic alleyways outside the old printing shops. Cold draft beer, spicy pork (dwaeji bulgogi), and fried garlic chicken. Gritty, authentic Seoul nightlife.
AccommodationBoutique Hotel / Hanok Guesthouse in Jongno or Anguk neighborhood (e.g., near Anguk Station, Line 3). Traditional Seoul experience.
Trip.com Booking
FoodLunch: Light meal near Changdeokgung or Ikseon-dong.
Dinner: Euljiro — cold draft beer, spicy pork (dwaeji bulgogi), and fried garlic chicken in the printing shop alleyways.

Practical Tips

  • BOOK Changdeokgung Secret Garden slots now — they release exactly 6 days in advance online and sell out instantly.
  • Taxi north of the river: COEX to Jongno/Anguk ~30 min. Drop bags, then head to Changdeokgung.
  • Euljiro: Line 2 to Euljiro 1-ga Station. The alleyways are behind the old printing shops — look for the narrow lanes with outdoor tables.
Day 11 — Premium Wagyu & The City Wall (Jul 13, Sun)
Walking / Subway Full day
TimeDurationActivity
Morning~1hSlightly slower morning. Coffee and pastry at a cafe around Anguk Station (e.g., Onion or Cafe Layered).
11:30–13:30~2hMajang Meat Market (마장도축시장). Walk the ground-floor stalls, buy a beautiful cut of 1++ grade Hanwoo beef, and take it upstairs to a grill restaurant for a heavy, world-class lunch. This is where Seoul locals buy their premium beef.
15:30–18:30~3hInwangsan City Wall (인왕산 성곽). Begin the hike up from the base of Inwangsan as the sun begins to set. Reach the viewing points for perfect juxtaposition of the ancient stone fortress against the hyper-modern Seoul skyline. N Seoul Tower is visible from the wall — great photo opportunity.
19:30–21:30~2hGwangjang Market (광장시장). Sit at the center stalls for freshly fried mung bean pancakes (bindaetteok) and makgeolli. Netflix-featured stalls.
AccommodationBoutique Hotel / Hanok Guesthouse in Jongno or Anguk neighborhood (final night)
FoodBreakfast: Coffee and pastry at a cafe around Anguk Station (e.g., Onion or Cafe Layered).
Lunch: Majang Meat Market — 1++ grade Hanwoo beef, grilled at the restaurant upstairs.
Dinner: Gwangjang Market — bindaetteok (mung bean pancakes) and makgeolli.

Practical Tips

  • Majang Meat Market: Subway to Majang Station (Line 4). Allow 1.5–2 hours for the full experience.
  • Inwangsan City Wall: Start hiking around 3:30–4:00 PM for sunset views. N Seoul Tower is visible from the wall — great photo opportunity.
  • Gwangjang Market: 5-min walk from Jongno 5-ga Station (Lines 1, 3, 5). Arrive by 7:00 PM for the best atmosphere.
